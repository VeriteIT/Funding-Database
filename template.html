📖 What this guide is

This guide brings together financing and funding options relevant to Sri Lanka’s Textile & Clothing (T&C) sector, with a focus on small and medium enterprises (SMEs). It is produced under the International Trade Centre’s Global Textiles and Apparel Programme (GTEX). It maps __COUNT_TOTAL__ products from __COUNT_INSTITUTIONS__ institutions — banks, finance companies, development and government bodies, and investors — and tags each one so you can quickly see whether it fits your business, what it costs, and how to apply.

🚀 The four sections
  1. 1Overview — a snapshot of the whole guide: totals and charts showing the mix of lenders, product types, purposes and business sizes. Use the “I need financing for…” shortcuts to jump straight into matching products.
  2. 2Finance Finder — the main tool. Narrow products down by what you need the money for, business size, product type, repayment period, lender, institution type, and whether the product is collateral-free, women-led, concessionary or green. Click any row to open full details, or use the Product page link to visit the funder’s website.
  3. 3All Products — the complete list. Sort by any column, use the filter dropdown under each heading (like a spreadsheet), or search across everything.
  4. 4Finance Explained — plain-language definitions of every product type, purpose, institution type and key term, each with a Textile & Clothing example. New to finance? Start here.

🔍 How institutions were selected

The database was built through a structured mapping of Sri Lanka’s supply-side financing landscape, covering seven categories of financial and funding entities:

  • Licensed commercial banks
  • Non-bank financial institutions registered with the Central Bank of Sri Lanka (CBSL)
  • Microfinance institutions registered with the CBSL
  • Development banks
  • Government funding bodies
  • Impact investors and venture capital providers
  • Multilateral institutions and development finance institutions (DFIs)

The defining inclusion criterion was an active online presence. Product information was drawn directly from each institution’s own official website (extracted on 9 July 2026), so every detail — product names, eligibility conditions, financing terms and applicability to Textile & Clothing (T&C) SMEs — comes from authoritative, first-party disclosures rather than secondary or intermediary references, preserving accuracy and verifiability.

Institutions without a discernible online presence, or whose websites did not publish sufficient product-level information, were omitted, because their offerings could not be reliably verified through this website-based extraction. Restricting the scope to funders with a web presence is a deliberate trade-off in favour of data reliability and consistency — while acknowledging that a small number of entities operating solely through offline or relationship-based channels fall outside this exercise.

ℹ️ Data, currency & a note of caution

Product information is drawn from each funder’s own official website and is current as of 9 July 2026; terms change often, so always confirm the latest details with the institution before applying. This guide is an information resource, not financial advice or a recommendation, and eligibility for any product is decided by the funder.
